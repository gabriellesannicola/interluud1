## Design Context

### Users
High-achieving professionals — executives, founders, lawyers, creatives — navigating divorce, separation, or breakup. They arrive emotionally depleted but professionally capable. They're used to being in control; losing it is unfamiliar and uncomfortable. They don't want to be fixed — they want to be equipped. They come to interluud when they've realised that processing feelings alone won't get them to where they need to go.

### Brand Personality
**Warm & curious meets strategic precision.** Gabrielle asks the right questions and makes people feel deeply heard — then gives them a roadmap. interluud is neither therapist nor life coach: it's a coach-strategist, a rare pairing of emotional intelligence and structured thinking.

Three words: **Grounded. Precise. Human.**

Emotional goal: Visitors should feel — immediately — that they are understood, that complexity is welcome here, and that there is a clear path forward. Not hope, not comfort: *clarity*.

Voice: Warm and curious. Asks before advising. Makes people feel heard before giving direction. Direct without being cold. Strategic without being clinical.

Transformation: Clients leave interluud rebuilt from the inside out — stronger sense of self, clearer values, more confident decisions across all of life. Not just through the transition; *changed by it*.

### Aesthetic Direction
**"Measured clarity"** — the intersection of a high-end architecture firm and a wellness brand. Warm but exact. Calming but not soft. Editorial calm meets intelligent precision.

The name "interluud" is the key: **liminal transformation** — the in-between state where real change happens. Not where you were, not yet where you're going. Every design decision should evoke that threshold quality: the charged stillness of a page turning.

**References**: High-end architecture firms, editorial magazines (The Cut, Kinfolk), premium professional services.
**Anti-references**: Headspace pastel blobs, corporate blue/grey grids, glassmorphism, neon, purple-to-cyan gradients, stock therapy imagery (candles, rocks, white rooms), anything that reads as "AI-generated bold".

**Palette**: Warm cream base + deep warm charcoal + burnt sienna accent. Sienna is the sole accent — unexpected for this category, signals warmth and confidence without softness. Full values in `.claude/DESIGN.md`.
**Typography**: Cormorant (editorial serif, emotional weight) + DM Sans (precision body). Never system fonts.
**Layout**: Asymmetric, left-aligned, editorial proportions. Generous whitespace signals unhurried premium — not emptiness.

### Design Principles

1. **Clarity over comfort** — Every element earns its place by reducing cognitive load or building confidence. Remove anything decorative that doesn't serve meaning.

2. **Strategic warmth** — The design should mirror the brand voice: curious, human, heard — but with direction and structure underneath. Warmth is earned through precision, not softness.

3. **Liminal by design** — interluud exists in the threshold. Compositions carry that quality: movement without chaos, pause without stagnation. Asymmetry, generous negative space, and typographic restraint all serve this.

4. **The premium of restraint** — One accent colour. One typographic contrast. One hero moment per section. Bold design doesn't mean more — it means more intentional.

5. **Hierarchy that leads** — High-achieving clients are decisive when given clear information. Guide the eye without ambiguity: headline → context → action. Never three things competing at once.
